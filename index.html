A Legacy That Changed India
Grand Master
C. Hanumanth Rao
Founder · Budokan Karate-Do India · 1978

In the annals of Indian martial arts, few names carry the weight and reverence of Grand Master C. Hanumanth Rao. A man of extraordinary vision, discipline, and devotion, he did not merely teach karate — he carried an entire tradition across oceans and planted it on Indian soil.

His journey began under the direct guidance of Grand Master Chew Choo Soot, the Malaysian pioneer who founded the Budokan Karate-Do system in 1966. Recognising the depth and authenticity of this art form, Grand Master Hanumanth Rao made it his life's mission to bring Budokan to India — and in 1978, he did exactly that. He became the first person to formally establish Budokan Karate-Do in India, opening the doors of this discipline to a generation of students who would never have encountered it otherwise.

His contribution went far beyond technique. He built an entire national infrastructure — training instructors, conducting gradings, establishing affiliated academies, and nurturing champions at state and national levels. His academies became temples of discipline, where students were shaped not just as fighters, but as human beings of character and integrity.

A true Budokan practitioner does not seek to defeat others. He seeks to master himself — and in doing so, he elevates everyone around him.

— Grand Master C. Hanumanth Rao

Among the many whom Grand Master Hanumanth Rao mentored and inspired is Sensei P. Ganesh Nayak — the founder of Nayak Budokan Karate Academy, Hyderabad. Sensei Ganesh Nayak trained directly under Grand Master Hanumanth Rao, absorbing not only the technical mastery of Budokan but more importantly its philosophy — that karate begins with respect and ends with character.

Today, through NBKA and its branches across Hyderabad, the legacy of Grand Master C. Hanumanth Rao lives on — in every kata performed, every belt earned, and every young student who bows before stepping onto the dojo floor.
