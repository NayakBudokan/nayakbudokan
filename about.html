About NBKA

Nayak Budokan Karate Academy (NBKA) was established with a clear vision to spread the true values of traditional karate, discipline, and self-defense. The academy was founded by P. Ganesh Nayak along with his dedicated team, under the guidance, blessings, and mentorship of Grand Master C. Hanumanth Rao.

NBKA was not created merely as a training center, but as a place where students learn discipline, respect, confidence, and strong moral values. The academy follows traditional karate principles that focus on character development before physical combat.

Budokan Lineage and Legacy

Nayak Budokan Karate Academy proudly follows the Budokan Karate-Do system. Budokan Karate-Do International was founded in 1966 by Grand Master Chew Choo Soot in Malaysia. The system emphasizes discipline, strength, self-defense, and moral values.

Budokan Karate-Do was introduced in India in 1978 by Grand Master C. Hanumanth Rao, who was personally trained and mentored by Grand Master Chew Choo Soot. His dedication laid a strong foundation for Budokan Karate across India.
